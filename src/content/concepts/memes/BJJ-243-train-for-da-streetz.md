None of that modern Jiu Jitsu Bullshit, we train for mortal kombat.

There's a grain of truth in there, it is interesting for sure for have some awareness of where punches and kicks could come from (if anything, the opponent being in a good enough posture to throw punches and kicks means his posture is not compromised, which is not a good sign)

But if that becomes the entire persona, it can be a tired look : WE do the real stuff, THEY do bullshit.

"None of that competition crap! they don't even know I could punch/eye gouge them..."  yeah. I like the idea that BJJ is ALSO helpful for self-defense, but it can be offputting if the badassery is overdone.
