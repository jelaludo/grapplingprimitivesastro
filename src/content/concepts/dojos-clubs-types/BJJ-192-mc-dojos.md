Mc Dojos exist on a spectrum, we can imagine the key dimensions of a training environment, and that McDojos are deficient in most of them.  Not necessarily scams, just lacking.

And obsessed with decorum and rank usually : BOW TO YOUR SENSEI!

meh.

Maybe we can think of it in terms of Aristotle's Golden Mean.
Courage is great, but too much and you have recklessness, too little and it's cowardice.
generosity, wastefulness, stinginess
ambition, sloth, greed
humor, boringness, absurdity
composure, irritabgility, apathy
etc.


Challenging training:
Excess: Recklessly unsafe, aggressive
Deficiency: Unrealistic samurai roleplay


Physical conditioning:
Excess: Overtraining, leading to burnout or injury
Deficiency: Insufficient conditioning, resulting in limited progress


Technical instruction:
Excess: Overemphasis on complex techniques, neglecting fundamentals
Deficiency: Oversimplification of techniques, limiting effectiveness


Sparring intensity:
Excess: Full-contact sparring without proper safety measures or supervision
Deficiency: Limited or no contact sparring, preventing the development of practical skills


Teaching methodology:
Excess: Rigid, dogmatic instruction, stifling creativity and growth
Deficiency: Unstructured, laissez-faire teaching, resulting in confusion and lack of direction


Respect and discipline:
Excess: Authoritarian, overly strict environment, discouraging open communication
Deficiency: Disorganized, chaotic atmosphere, lacking in respect for tradition and hierarchy


Focus on tradition and history:
Excess: Overemphasis on historical aspects, neglecting practical application
Deficiency: Ignoring cultural context, reducing martial arts to mere physical activity

Inclusivity and community:
Excess: Overemphasis on group dynamics, neglecting individual needs and development
Deficiency: Excessive focus on individual competition, undermining the sense of community and support

Balancing self-defense and personal development:
Excess: Sole focus on self-defense techniques, ignoring character growth and discipline
Deficiency: Overemphasis on personal development, neglecting practical self-defense skills

Competition and performance:
Excess: Overemphasis on winning tournaments, fostering a win-at-all-costs mentality
Deficiency: Disregarding the value of competition, missing opportunities for growth and skill development
