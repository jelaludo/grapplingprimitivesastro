Unforced Errors in Tennis. Blunder in Chess.

Sometimes it pays to be reactive rather than proactive. 
To let them make mistakes that you can exploit.

You set it up either through bait, frustration, or a dilemma.

Say you're in a strong base, 50/50 standing as you're both in a position to go for an Osotogari outside leg sweep, if you moving first means compromising your stance, which they could use, sometimes it's best to wait for them to commit and react.

It helps to know the handful of potential most likely outcome from a given position.

Don't interrupt an opponent making a mistake.

This is not a hard and fast rule, don't wait *all the time ffs.

sometimes it makes sense.
