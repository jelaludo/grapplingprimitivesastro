OODA Loop: Observe, Orient, Decide, Act.

It’s a decision making model from fighter pilot John Boyd. You constantly cycle through those steps faster than your opponent to stay ahead.

In grappling, it’s basically what we're trying to do every roll:

Observe: Where are their hands? Weight? Hips?

Orient: Based on their game, intent, and your game, what are your options?

Decide: Pick the next sequence.

Act: Go for it.

The trick is: if you loop faster than your partner, you’re two steps ahead while they’re still figuring things out.

Fast OODA = dominate the pace. 
Slow OODA = get smeshed.

"win battles they don't know they're fighting"
