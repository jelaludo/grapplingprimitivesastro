Your limbs are handles for the opponent.

The more you extend those handles, the longer of a lever you provide the opponent with.

"Elbows in"

Stability increases as your limbs remain close to your center of gravity. Extended limbs act as levers for your opponent and weaken your base.

“The closer the limbs, the stronger the structure.”

Turtling with elbows tight = strong defense (in a grappling scenario without soccer kicks)

Posting too far = easy arm drag

Framing too long = underhook bait

Posting wide on top = sweep bait

In open guard butterly, Marcelo recommends to never have your feet extended further away from your hands.

The more compact you are, the harder it is to penetrate your defenses.
