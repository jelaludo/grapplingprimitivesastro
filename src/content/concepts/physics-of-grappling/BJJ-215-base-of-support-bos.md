In sports science, the Base of Support (BoS) refers to the area beneath a person that includes every point of contact with the supporting surface. A wider BOS generally equates to greater stability, while a smaller one BOS leads to increased mobility, or vulnerability to falling.

Judo throws and sweeps can be visualized as bring the opponent's CoG outside of their BoS.

Center of Gravity
Base of Support

Kuzushi brings their CoG to the tipping point
Kake (execution) makes it flip over.

Crouching, widening stance, retracting limbs; all these influence the BoS.

I've seen advanced studies of Rugby/American Football with the BoS concept being used for tackles and mobility to avoid tackles.
