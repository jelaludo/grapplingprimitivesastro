Every joint in your body is a lever, and grappling is basically the art of using them well. The basic idea is simple: the further from the fulcrum (the joint) you grip, the more force you can generate with less effort. That's why an armbar works so much better when you control near the wrist instead of near the elbow. Same force from your hips, but way more breaking pressure on their arm.

Kimuras, americanas, knee bars, toe holds. They're all lever systems. You're creating a mechanical advantage by controlling the long end of the lever while pinning the short end. When someone says "use technique, not strength," they're really saying "use better leverage." A 130-pound person with great lever placement can generate more joint pressure than a 200-pound person muscling it from a bad angle.
