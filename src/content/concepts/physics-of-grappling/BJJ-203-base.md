Base and Base of Support are related but slightly different.

Base as "Resistance to linear displacement" might be a good physics framing.

In grappling having "base" is a qualitative term referring to someone's ability to maintain posture, resist being moved, and apply force effectively.

It cncludes BoS but also posture, weight distribution, and muscular tension.

“Good base” = hard to sweep, hard to move, stable but dynamic.

Often trained/felt more than defined.

Coach: “Widen your base!”, “Find your base before attacking!”

Less concerned with the shape of contact points, more with functional resistance under stress.
