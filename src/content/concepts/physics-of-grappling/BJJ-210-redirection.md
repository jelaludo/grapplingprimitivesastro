Redirection is the principle of taking force that's already coming at you and guiding it somewhere else instead of meeting it head-on. This is the heart of so many throws and sweeps. You don't stop their push; you step aside and let it carry them past you, or steer it straight into the mat.

Think about a basic tomoe nage. Someone drives into you hard, and instead of bracing against it, you drop underneath them and use their own forward momentum to launch them overhead. You barely added any force. You just changed where their existing force went.

The same idea shows up in guard work. If someone is driving forward to pass, a well-timed hip bump or elevator hook redirects that energy into a sweep. They did most of the work for you. The best grapplers are constantly looking for incoming energy they can borrow and reroute. It's way more efficient than generating your own force from scratch.
