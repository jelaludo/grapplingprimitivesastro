Impulse is force multiplied by the time you apply it, and it determines how much you actually change someone's momentum. This is why a quick explosive bridge feels completely different from sustained grinding pressure, even though both can get you a sweep.

A hard, fast bridge (high force, short time) creates a sharp impulse that pops someone off balance before they can react. Sustained heavy pressure (lower force, longer time) creates impulse more gradually, slowly eroding their position until it collapses. Both approaches change their momentum, just on different timescales.

Choosing which type of impulse to use is a real skill. Explosive impulse works great against bigger, heavier opponents who are hard to move slowly. They're braced for grinding pressure, so a sudden pop catches them off guard. Meanwhile, steady pressure wears down smaller, faster opponents who can handle quick bursts but struggle under relentless weight over time.
