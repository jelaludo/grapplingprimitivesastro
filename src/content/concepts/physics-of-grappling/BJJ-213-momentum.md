Momentum is mass times velocity, which means a heavier person moving slowly and a lighter person moving quickly can carry the same amount of it. Understanding this helps you figure out your best strategy based on your body type and the situation.

If you're heavy, you can generate momentum just by leaning and shifting your weight. You don't need to be fast; your mass does the work. This is why pressure passers can be so effective at higher weight classes. They're just slowly converting their mass into momentum that grinds through the guard.

If you're lighter, your path to momentum is through speed. Fast entries, quick scrambles, rapid direction changes. You're trading the mass advantage for a velocity advantage. A 140-pound person shooting a fast double leg can absolutely have more momentum than a 220-pound person slowly stepping forward.

The real game is knowing when to use weight-based momentum versus speed-based momentum, and recognizing which one your opponent is using so you can counter it.
