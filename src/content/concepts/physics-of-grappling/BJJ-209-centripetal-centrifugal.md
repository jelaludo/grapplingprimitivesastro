When you move in a circular path around your opponent, centripetal force is what keeps you on that arc, pulling you inward toward the center. The reaction you feel pulling outward is the centrifugal effect. Both matter in grappling more than people realize.

Berimbolo entries are a great example. You're spinning underneath someone, and the circular motion generates forces that are genuinely hard to counter with straight-line resistance. Spinning attacks, cartwheeling passes, and circular footwork in wrestling all use this principle. The person in the center has to rotate to track you, while you can use the momentum of your orbit to chain into the next move.

This also explains why spinning out of bad positions works. If someone has a tight grip on you but you rotate around the contact point, the centrifugal tendency helps you break free. It's easier to escape along a curve than to pull straight back against their grip.
