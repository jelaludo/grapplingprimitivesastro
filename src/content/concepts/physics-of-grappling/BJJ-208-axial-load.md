Axial load is force applied along the length of a bone or limb, like pushing straight down a pipe. When you stack someone in closed guard, driving their knees toward their face, you're compressing their spine axially. It makes them miserable and takes away their hip movement, which is exactly why it's such an effective passing concept.

You also see axial loading in can openers, certain neck cranks, and any time you drive your weight straight through someone's skeletal structure. A good stack pass works because the human spine doesn't handle compressive loads well when it's flexed.

One important note: axial loads to the neck and spine can genuinely injure people if applied carelessly. There's a reason can openers are banned at white belt in most competitions. Use this principle to create pressure and control, but be thoughtful about where you're directing that force, especially in training.
