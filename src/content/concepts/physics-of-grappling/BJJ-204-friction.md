Friction is the force that keeps you from sliding off your opponent, and it quietly shapes almost everything in grappling. The single biggest difference between gi and no-gi isn't the techniques; it's the friction. A cotton gi creates enormous friction, letting you maintain grips and slow the game down. Take it away, add some sweat, and suddenly everything is slippery and fast.

Sweat is basically a lubricant that reduces friction as the round goes on. This is why no-gi scrambles get wilder in the later minutes. Positions that felt secure when you were both dry start falling apart. Smart grapplers adjust their game as friction changes throughout a round.

The mat surface matters too. A sticky mat gives your feet more purchase for takedowns and passing. A slick mat makes knee slides easier but sprawling harder. Even skin texture plays a role. Shaved legs in no-gi are genuinely harder to grip than hairy ones. Every surface interaction in grappling is a friction problem, whether you realize it or not.
