Fast, experience-based rules-of-thumb used to make real-time decisions under pressure. Heuristics reduce cognitive load by replacing exhaustive analysis with pattern recognition and tactical defaults.

Heuristics ≠ techniques. They’re decision frameworks that reduce chaos into action.

They should be taught alongside techniques as meta-skills.

Good heuristics are adaptive, not rigid.

Heuristics are simplification.  All simplifications are wrong, but some are useful.

Why Heuristics Matter
Grappling is dynamic and chaotic.

Time for deep reasoning = zero.

Heuristics enable speed with survivability—even if not always optimal, they are robust.

Examples of BJJ Heuristics
Heuristic;Application
“Frame when you can’t move, move when you can’t frame”;Guides positional defense.

“Two on one beats one”; Arm drag, grip breaking, shoulder control.

“Head higher, hips heavier”;Passing posture, pressure principles.

“If they post, you sweep”;Cue for transitioning from submission to reversal.

“Control the inside space”;Dominant frames, underhooks, collar ties.

“If you can see the back, take it”;Positional hierarchy—reward opportunity.

“When in doubt, fight for the underhook”	Universal in scrambling and control.

“If they push, pull. If they pull, push.”	;Kuzushi and tempo control.

Heuristics in BJJ are mental guardrails. They trade perfect choices for fast, repeatable, mostly-right actions under pressure. They're how instincts are built—and how instinct wins.

You use heuristics to frame your training, and hopefully once they're integrated you don't need to think about them anymore.  Unconscious Competence.
