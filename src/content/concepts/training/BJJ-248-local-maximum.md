In a skills acquisition curve, one grappler might reach a local peak, (higher than some peers who started around the same time), but to reach higher in the next mountain, they might have to go down a notch once before climbing higher.

maybe they muscle through too much, and that reaches a plateau once they encounter people with the technical levels to deal with it.

Unlearn to learn.
go back down, come back up.

metaphor is roughly that climbing in a fog, you reach a hill that looks like the peak, but when the fog clears you realize that there's a much higher peak in the distance.

it's a math concept, also used in computer science and evolutionary biology, like the Galapagos maybe, where some organisms reached a local peak, but are not quite fit for the wider world.
