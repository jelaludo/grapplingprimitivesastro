Too focused on "winning" training rounds is not the healthiest mindset. Even worse is feeling bad for losing in training rounds. We're here to develop skills.

**Train to experiment and improve. Compete to win.**

It's fine to bring the heat and keep the room realistic with some intensity. It's detrimental to obsess over winning in training and celebrating (even just to yourself) tapping your training partners.

If you do "win" a round, reflect on what went right and what went wrong, not "I'm better, fuck yeah!".

Training : Mastery
Competition : Performance

If every moment you roll you focus on winning, you'll narrow your behavior — no risk, no exploring the edge of your comfort, less growth.

Short-term ego boost, long-term stagnation.
