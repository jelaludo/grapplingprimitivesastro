King Mithridates VI built immunity to poison by taking small doses daily, gradually increasing the amount until lethal doses couldn't touch him. Defensive grappling works the same way.

You don't learn to escape back control by avoiding it. You learn by putting yourself there deliberately, at low intensity, with a cooperative partner, and working the problem. Then you increase resistance. Then you try it against tougher opponents. Then in competition.

The two non-negotiables: willful exposure (you choose to be in the bad spot) and graduated dosing (start cooperative, build to live). Skipping the gradual part leads to getting smashed and learning nothing. Skipping the exposure part means you're avoiding the positions you most need to practice.

This applies to every scary position. Mount escapes, leg lock defense, back survival. Start with a partner who lets you work, add resistance in steps, and eventually pressure-test it against the killers in your gym. That's how comfort in bad positions is actually built.
