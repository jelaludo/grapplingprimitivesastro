Pivots in biomechanics are used to generate force and evade force.  It's essentially a specific use of a fulcrum, used to move rather than apply torque.  Rotation, mobility, redirection.
Find pivot points to  rotate, change angles, escape, or position yourself more efficiently.


Dual Use of Pivots/Fulcrums in Biomechanics
For Offense; Leverage & Force Application 
Pivot points (joints, grips) enable torque by creating rotational force around them.
Used to amplify power (e.g., armbars, levers, throws).

For Defense; Escape & Energy Redirection
Pivot points (body contact points) act as anchors for rotating the body away from danger.
Used to evade force efficiently (e.g., hip escapes, granby rolls).
