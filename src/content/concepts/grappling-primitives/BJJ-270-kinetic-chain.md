Every powerful movement in grappling follows the same pattern: force starts at the ground, travels through the feet, up the legs, through the hips and core, and out to the limbs. This is the kinetic chain.

A bridge isn't a back exercise; it's a ground-reaction-force exercise where the feet push into the mat and the hips express that force upward. A good sweep doesn't start with the arms pulling; it starts with hip displacement that the arms merely direct. When the chain is connected (skeleton aligned, core engaged, force flowing through structure rather than muscles), you feel heavy and efficient. When it's broken (loose core, bent spine, arms working alone), you feel weak and get tired fast.

The old martial arts maxim applies perfectly: power is rooted in the feet, directed by the waist, and expressed in the hands. Every time a technique feels like it takes too much effort, check the chain. Somewhere between the ground and your point of contact, there's a leak. Find it, reconnect it, and the technique gets easy.
