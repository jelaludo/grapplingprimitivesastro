The "other limb" principle. In any limb attack (armbar, kimura, heel hook), the free limb is what decides whether the attack works. 90% of leg lock defense comes from the other leg. A free hand grabbing your gi pants can shut down a kimura. In RDLR, controlling their free posting hand is the key to denying their next move.

Think about a standard armbar from guard. You isolate one arm, great. But if their other hand is free to grip, stack, or posture, that armbar is going nowhere. The same logic applies on defense: when someone attacks your right arm, your left hand is your best rescue tool.

General rule: once you commit a limb to an attack, immediately account for their free limb. And vice versa in defense, your free limb is your best tool. The players who look effortless on offense are usually the ones who quietly neutralize the secondary limb before they even start the main attack.
