Most people think of control as pinning things in place: hold the hips, hold the shoulders. But the strongest control comes from working on a diagonal. Far shoulder plus near hip creates a torsional force through the torso that the spine has very little ability to resist.

This shows up everywhere. In back control, the spiral strategy uses exactly this. In side control, the classic crossface plus underhook is a diagonal. In mount, controlling one shoulder while trapping the opposite hip prevents the upa.

The diagonal works because it attacks the spine's weakest axis of rotation. A straight compression (pushing down) can be bridged. A lateral push can be shrimped. But a diagonal twist? The body has to solve two problems at once, and that's much harder.

Next time you're in a control position, check whether your pressure runs on a diagonal or parallel. If you're pushing straight down or straight sideways, you're leaving escape routes open. Shift to opposite corners and feel the difference immediately.
