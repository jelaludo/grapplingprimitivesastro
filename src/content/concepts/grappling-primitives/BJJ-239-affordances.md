Affordance comes from design theory (Gibson/Norman) and describes the action possibilities that an object, surface, or situation “offers” to an agent. 

In product design, a door handle affords pulling, a flat plate affords pushing, a knob affords twisting, a switch affords flicking.

Crucially, it’s about what an observer sees as possible (perceived affordance), not just the theoretical possibilities.

I see it as another concept for "visual triggers".

Open armpit : affords a underhook
Flared elbow : Kimura
Extended leg while on bottom, knee slice or back-step to saddle.
Weight shift back : ankle pick, single leg


it's not just what is physically possible, it's what your trained perception spots immediately as a viable action.

I'm on the bottom and spot a space in their armpit? shoulder crunch.

etc.

train to multiply your affordances.

Other terms for similar ideas, more or less pompous : 
- triggers
- invitations
- cues
- handles
- Window of opportunities
- openings
