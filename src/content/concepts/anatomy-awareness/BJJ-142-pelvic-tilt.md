The sacrum's orientation can be 1) tucking (posterior tilt) or 2) arching (anterior tilt).

This influences pelvic posture and spinal mechanics. A neutral or slightly posterior tilt stabilizes the core, reinforces base in guard, and supports safe spinal stacking. Awareness of sacral tilt is foundational for resisting sweeps or generating hip motion.

There's a "trick" in close guard whereby engaging the pelvis can mostly negate attempts to break you forward with strong harmstring leg pulls.
