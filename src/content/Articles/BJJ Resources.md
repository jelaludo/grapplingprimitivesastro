_"The tools for learning are ubiquitous, it's the desire and discipline to learn that are lacking"_  
  
That's a quote about education in general in an age of endless online resources, and it also applies to learning Jiu Jitsu.  
  
I will list some of the many resources I've encountered and recommend.  
  
Ryan Hall : One of the first to dive deep into analyzing and systemizing Jiu Jitsu teaching.  
  
[Grappler's Guide](https://grapplersguide.com) by Jason Scully  
  
[BJJ Globetrotters](https://www.bjjglobetrotters.com/) by Christian Gaugart  
  
[Jiu Jitsu Brotherhood](https://www.jiujitsubrotherhood.com) by Nic Gregoriades, Roger Gracie's first Black Belt

The Canadians :  
[Grapplearts](https://www.grapplearts.com/) by Stephan Kesting  
[RVV](https://www.youtube.com/user/Bageluh) "The Science behind your jiu jitsu"  
[BJJ Mental Models](https://bjjmentalmodels.com/) by Steve and [Matt](https://youtu.be/nES1d8EjNTc?t=417) Kwan  
  
[BJJ Fanatics](https://bjjfanatics.com/) : Amazing accumulation of knowledge there, but fanatics get addicted, and I've already accumulated over a 1,000 points there, which means I spent over $1,000 on videos.
