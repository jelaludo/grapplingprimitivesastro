**_What's the most important thing to focus on at each belt level?　
各ベルトレベルで最もフォーカスすべきものは何ですか  
_**  
白：リラックス　　White: Relax  
青：リラックス　　Blue: Relax  
紫：リラックス　　Purple: Relax  
茶：リラックス　　Brown: Relax  
黒：学び始める　　Black: Start Learning.

This was the tongue-in-cheek answer that ex-Navy Seals commander and BJJ addict black belt Jocko Willink gave in a [reddit AMA](https://www.reddit.com/r/IAmA/comments/75qdfl/im_jocko_willink_retired_navy_seal_officer_author/).  
その回答がジョッコ・ウィリンク氏からきた、元海軍特殊部隊ネイビーシールズの元司令官と柔術黒帯。  
  
It's a bit tongue-in-cheek but it makes the point.  
ちょっとふざけているけど、意図が通じます。  
  
There seems to be some science backing up the idea that relaxed minds are better at receiving new information.  
新しい情報を受け入れるにはリラックスした状態の方が望ましいという発想を裏付ける科学実験があるようです。  
  
A deep topic to be frequently revisited.  
  
[This video](https://youtu.be/7kxaasAXIqc?t=1856) makes the point that weird attacks and positions have their place in BJJ, because they can potentially confuse the opponent, and simply because it's playful. (by Christian Graugart from BJJ Globetrotters)  
  
Lethargic  
Relaxed  
Tense  
  
There's a golden-mean to be found, a spot where your frames are robust enough to allow you to _prevent unnecessary muscles from firing_, which makes you more apt to react to subtle cues in your opponent's movements.  
  
Overcooked, al dente, undercooked.  
生すぎ、アルデンテ、ゆで過ぎ  
  
Firm but not tense  
Relaxed but not sluggish  
しっかりした状態ですが、力みはしない。  
リラックスした状態ですが、緩慢でもない。  
  
  
Finding the right amount of relaxation under stress is a never-ending pursuit, and it's probably not a steady-state either.  
ストレスを受けながら、適切な量のリラクゼーションを見つけることは終わりのない課題であり、おそらく定常状態でもありません。

  
_Active relaxation_ ?  
動的なリラクゼーション
